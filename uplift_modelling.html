# Cell 3.5  Propensity Score Estimation
from sklearn.linear_model import LogisticRegression

ps_model = LogisticRegression(max_iter=200)
ps_model.fit(X, t)
df_ab["propensity"] = ps_model.predict_proba(X)[:, 1]

# Cell 3.6  Inverse Probability Weighting (IPW)
ps = df_ab["propensity"].clip(0.01, 0.99)
w_treat = 1 / ps
w_ctrl = 1 / (1 - ps)

# 
w_treat /= np.mean(w_treat[t == 1])
w_ctrl /= np.mean(w_ctrl[t == 0])

Empirical Finding The uplift analysis reveals a strong inverse relationship between deposit sensitivity and baseline cancellation probability. Customers predicted to be most affected by deposits (high-uplift) actually show the lowest observed cancellation rates.

Business Insight

Require deposits from low-sensitivity customers (they cancel more).

Waive deposits for high-sensitivity customers (theyre stable but dislike friction).

After high-sensitivity users book, avoid post-booking offerstheyre prone to overthinking, which can increase cancellation.

Interpretation Deposit mechanisms serve as a selection filter, not a deterrent. They separate stable, rational customers from flexible, impulsive ones.
